Here is one of Helene's Poems

Wake up at four I can’t go back to bed.
My sleep’s not strong and neither is my head.
Stand up and stretch and put my slippers on,
My coffee pot whistles, unlike my yawn.

Six cups, two eggs and toast covered with spread.
Some happy dreams go bad, so did my bread.
In my old fridge a slice of cake left-over,
I keep chewing its mouthfuls like a prayer.

It’s eight, but dark. September’s clouds betrayed
My shirt, my skirt; my day can’t be delayed.
I shut the door and step out in the rain.
It rusts my skin, watering it in vain.

At ten, night time, I close my puffy eyes.
The water in my bath is warm and wise,
My eyelids’ screen just dappled, still and red.
I lie and I remember what we prayed

For in the mid of the summer,
That we could someday see each other
Again. Which is not happening.
So at midnight, I’m there again, waiting.
